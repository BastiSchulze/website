Lecturing

I'm lecturing the following courses within the Department of Engineering Science.

B14 Estimation and Inference

This course provides an introduction to probability theory, including various properties of distributions and their representation as belief networks. It covers maximum likelihood estimation and compares against Bayesian inference. The course concludes by introducing decision theory and classification. Read more.

C24 Advanced Probability Theory

This course is really less esoteric than its name might suggest, providing a grounded review of probabilistic tools relevant for many applications in data science and predictive analytics. In particular, the course will cover model comparison, the value of information, Gaussian processes, and approximate inference (in the form of Laplace's method and Markov Chain Monte Carlo). Read more.

Data, Estimation and Inference

This course will be taught within the AIMS CDT. A superposition of the courses above, covering an introduction to probability theory, including various properties of distributions and their representation as belief networks. It covers maximum likelihood estimation and compares against Bayesian inference, before introducing decision theory and classification. The course continues by covering temporal filtering and Gaussian processes, and approximate inference (in the form of Laplace's method and Markov Chain Monte Carlo). Read more.
