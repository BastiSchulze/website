31 May 2014

R oman Garnett, Philipp Hennig and I had our Active Learning of Linear Embeddings for Gaussian Processes paper accepted at UAI 2014. The paper makes a couple of contributions, as follows.

  1. An approach to cheaply averaging (marginalising) over the hyperparameters of a Gaussian process (GP): the Marginal GP (MGP). Roman has released a MATLAB implementation of the MGP, built upon GPML.
  2. A practical means of working with GPs even in several hundred input dimensions.
Abstract below.
We propose an active learning method for discovering low- dimensional structure in high-dimensional Gaussian process (GP) tasks. Such problems are increasingly frequent and important, but have hitherto presented severe practical difficulties. We further introduce a novel technique for approximately marginalizing GP hyperparameters, yielding marginal predictions robust to hyperparameter mis-specification. Our method offers an efficient means of performing GP regression, quadrature, or Bayesian optimization in high-dimensional spaces.

2013

4th December 2013

We have a new workshop paper out: Raiders of the Lost Architecture: Kernels for Bayesian Optimization in Conditional Parameter Spaces describes how to use the covariance from A Kernel for Hierarchical Parameter Spaces to perform Bayesian optimisation over structures with differing numbers of parameters. As a motivating example, we consider the optimisation of the parameters of a deep neural network, where the number of parameters will depend on the number of layers chosen.

13th November 2013

We have two new pre-prints up on arXiv. Firstly, Active Learning of Linear Embeddings for Gaussian Processes gives an approach to working with Gaussian processes even in very high dimensional input spaces. It also gives an approximate means of marginalising the hyperparameters of a Gaussian process.

Secondly, A Kernel for Hierarchical Parameter Spaces describes an approach to building covariances for input spaces where the relevance of some inputs is dependent on the value of other inputs. We extend to the case in which there is a hierarchy of such dependencies, so that the relevance of inputs can be dependent on the value of inputs whose relevance is dependent on the value of other inputs.
